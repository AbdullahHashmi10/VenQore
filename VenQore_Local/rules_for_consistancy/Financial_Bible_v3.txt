  SECTION 0
  VERSION CHANGELOG
  Every correction from v1.0 to v2.0 — with the reasoning
Why Each Fix Was Necessary
🚫 FIX 1 — Manufacturing Labor Double-Count (B16)
THE BUG: v1.0 said: when adding labor to a production run, credit Cash or Bank directly.
  Problem: If your employees are salaried, the B7 Salary process already posts:
  DR 6100 Salary Expense / CR 2400 Salary Payable
  Then when you run a production job and credit Cash for 'labor', you are paying twice.
  The salary is already accrued as Salary Payable — it will be paid from there.
  Crediting cash AGAIN is a phantom outflow that never happened.
THE FIX: Two clear labor paths:
  Path A — External / Contract labor (you pay someone new for this job):
    DR 6410 Applied Manufacturing Labor  |  CR 1000/1010 Cash or Bank
  Path B — Internal / Salaried employees (already on payroll):
    DR 6410 Applied Manufacturing Labor  |  CR 2400 Salary Payable (allocation, not cash)
  Either way: DR 1100 Inventory Asset  |  CR 6410 Applied Manufacturing Labor  (capitalise)
  Net effect: labor cost moves into inventory — no phantom cash outflow for salaried staff.
🚫 FIX 2 — Opening Balance Missing Fixed Assets and Loans (B19 + COA)
THE BUG: v1.0 COA had no Fixed Asset or Accumulated Depreciation accounts.
  A business migrating with Rs. 1,000,000 in equipment and a Rs. 500,000 loan could not
  enter those balances. Account 7000 Opening Balance Equity could never net to zero.
  A Balance Sheet that doesn't balance on day one is useless.
THE FIX:
  Added to COA: 1500 Fixed Assets, 1510 Accumulated Depreciation (contra-asset)
  Updated B19 to include: equipment, vehicles, loans, owner capital as opening entries
  Added B23 Depreciation (periodic journal) and B24 Loan Drawdown (new transactions)
  Now 7000 can zero out correctly across all real-world migration scenarios.
🚫 FIX 3 — Party Balance Has No Caching Architecture (C16)
THE BUG: v1.0 said: read party balance directly from journal_items filtered by party_id.
  This is correct for data integrity but catastrophic for performance at scale.
  A customer with 5 years of transactions = millions of rows scanned on every page load.
  This query will time out and take down the application under real usage.
THE FIX: party_snapshots table defined (see Section 4 C16).
  The snapshot stores the pre-computed balance per party per account.
  It is rebuilt ONLY when a new journal entry with that party_id is posted.
  The ledger remains the single source of truth — snapshots are just a performance cache.
  If a snapshot is missing or stale, the system falls back to the live ledger query.
🚫 FIX 4 — Payment Allocation Vacuum (NEW TABLE)
THE BUG: v1.0 correctly posts B4 as: DR Cash / CR Accounts Receivable (1200).
  Financially correct. But: how does the system know WHICH invoice got paid?
  If customer owes Rs. 10,000 across 3 invoices and pays Rs. 4,000 — which invoice
  gets the 'Paid' or 'Partial' badge? The journal entry has no invoice-level data.
  Without this, aged receivables are wrong, party statements are wrong, and
  the payment_status badge that users rely on daily is completely broken.
THE FIX: payment_allocations table (see Section 4, new subsection).
  Columns: id, payment_journal_entry_id, sale_id (nullable), purchase_id (nullable),
           allocated_amount, created_at
  Every B4/B5 payment explicitly links to one or more invoices with the amounts.
  Aged receivables and payment badges all read from this table.
🚫 FIX 5 — FIFO Direction Contradiction (B10 vs E3)
THE BUG: v1.0 B10 said 'reduce from newest batch backwards (reverse FIFO)' for decreases.
  v1.0 E3 said 'deduct from oldest batch first' for sales.
  These contradict each other. Expired goods ARE the oldest goods.
  A write-off of spoiled stock should remove the oldest batch, not the newest.
  Using newest-first for adjustments while using oldest-first for sales means
  your COGS and inventory valuation diverge — the system is internally inconsistent.
THE FIX: One rule for ALL stock decreases (B1, B2, B8, B9, B10, B16, B22):
  ORDER BY inventory_batches.created_at ASC — oldest batch first, always.
  No exceptions. Sales, write-offs, adjustments, donations — all the same direction.

1.1  The Single Source Principle
Every financial number in AMD ERP has exactly one source of truth: the journal_items table, queried through the double-entry ledger. No number is stored as a mutable running total used for financial reporting. No report or dashboard widget calculates independently. If it is a financial figure, it comes from the ledger — or from a snapshot cache that is guaranteed to mirror the ledger.
🚫 What creates inconsistency — the anti-patterns
bank_accounts.current_balance  ← updated by hand in controllers (WRONG)
parties.current_balance         ← updated by controller arithmetic (WRONG)
Dashboard cash formula          ← custom maths duplicating the ledger (WRONG)
sales.payment_status            ← used in financial calculations (WRONG — badge only)
products.cost_price             ← used for COGS or inventory value (WRONG — overwrites)
✅ The one true pattern
Cash in Hand    = SUM(debit) - SUM(credit) FROM journal_items WHERE account_code = '1000'
Bank Balance    = SUM(debit) - SUM(credit) FROM journal_items WHERE account_code = '1010'
Receivables     = SUM(debit) - SUM(credit) FROM journal_items WHERE account_code = '1200'
Payables        = SUM(credit)- SUM(debit)  FROM journal_items WHERE account_code = '2000'
Party Balance   = party_snapshots.cached_balance (rebuilt from ledger on every new entry)
Inventory Value = SUM(remaining_qty × unit_cost) FROM inventory_batches WHERE remaining_qty &gt; 0

1.3  The Double-Entry Golden Rules
Rule
Stated Simply
Consequence if Violated
1. Every transaction has two sides
Every debit has an equal credit
Trial Balance won't balance — error detectable immediately
2. Debits must equal Credits
SUM(debit) = SUM(credit) on every journal entry — to the paisa
System REJECTS the save if this is not satisfied
3. Entries are never deleted
Wrong entry? Post a reversal. Never delete.
Broken audit trail, Trial Balance drift
4. Posting is atomic
Business record + journal entries in one DB::transaction()
Partial writes leave the books in an illegal state
5. Every entry has a reference
reference_type + reference_id links journal to source
Cannot trace back to the original event — audit fails
6. Debit = Asset/Expense up
Debit increases assets and expenses
Getting this backwards inverts all balances
7. Credit = Liability/Income/Equity up
Credit increases liabilities, income, equity
Fundamental — cannot be violated

  SECTION 3
  TRANSACTIONS
  B1–B24: Every event that moves money or stock — complete journal definitions
Every transaction MUST produce the journal entries shown. AccountingService::createEntry() must be called inside DB::transaction() alongside all other writes. The engine validates SUM(debit) = SUM(credit) before saving — it throws an exception and rolls back if not balanced.
🚫 Universal transaction rules
1.  Every transaction calls AccountingService::createEntry() — NO EXCEPTIONS
2.  All writes happen inside DB::transaction() — business record + journal entries together
3.  Journal entries are NEVER deleted — post reversals only
4.  Every journal entry carries: entry_date, reference_type, reference_id, party_id (if applicable)
5.  The engine validates SUM(debit) = SUM(credit) before saving — REJECT if not equal
B1  —  Sale
B1
Sale
WHAT
Customer receives products. Revenue earned. Stock leaves. Money collected immediately (cash) or owed later (credit).
JOURNAL
On posting the invoice
DR  1200  Accounts Receivable     = invoice_total  (what customer owes incl. tax)
CR  4000  Sales Revenue           = net_sales  (gross minus all discounts, EXCLUDING tax)
CR  2200  Sales Tax Payable       = total_tax  (government's share — not your revenue)
CR  4100  Other Income            = shipping_charges  (if any)
COGS — runs simultaneously via FifoService (oldest batch first)
DR  5000  Cost of Goods Sold      = FIFO cost of items sold  (from sale_item_batches)
CR  1100  Inventory Asset         = same FIFO cost  (stock value leaves)
For CASH sales — immediate settlement entry
DR  1000  Cash in Hand            = amount_received
CR  1200  Accounts Receivable     = amount_received  (clears the AR just created)
AFFECTS
Cash/Bank ↑ (cash sale)  |  Receivables ↑ (credit sale)  |  Revenue ↑  |  COGS ↑  |  Inventory ↓  |  Tax Payable ↑
RULE
NEVER include tax in Revenue (4000). Tax → 2200 Sales Tax Payable.
ALWAYS use FIFO batch cost from sale_item_batches.total_cogs — never products.cost_price.
payment_allocations: create a record linking this payment to this sale_id when cash is collected.
B2  —  POS Sale
B2
POS Sale (Point of Sale)
WHAT
Same as B1 but with split-payment support across multiple methods in a single transaction.
JOURNAL
Invoice entries: identical to B1
DR  1200  Accounts Receivable     = invoice_total
CR  4000  Sales Revenue           = net_sales
CR  2200  Sales Tax Payable       = total_tax
DR  5000  COGS                    = FIFO cost
CR  1100  Inventory Asset         = FIFO cost
Split payment settlement
DR  1000  Cash in Hand            = cash_portion
DR  1010  Bank Account            = card_or_bank_portion
CR  1200  Accounts Receivable     = total  (sum of all portions)
AFFECTS
Same as B1. Split payment debits multiple cash/bank accounts simultaneously.
RULE
Held / parked bills: zero journal entries until the bill is finalised and posted.
Change given is counter maths only — never a journal entry.
payment_allocations: link each portion to this sale_id.

B3
Purchase
WHAT

JOURNAL

B4

B7  —  Salary Payment
B7
Salary Payment
WHAT
Paying employees. Hits Salary Expense (6100), never Accounts Payable (2000). Two-step: accrue at period end, pay when due.
JOURNAL
Step 1 — Accrue salary (end of pay period, salary earned but not yet paid)
DR  6100  Salary Expense          = gross_salary
CR  2400  Salary Payable          = gross_salary
Step 2 — Pay the salary (actual payment date)
DR  2400  Salary Payable          = net_amount_paid
CR  1000  Cash in Hand            = net_amount_paid  (cash)
  OR
CR  1010  Bank Account            = net_amount_paid  (bank transfer)
Deductions logic (advances, fines)
Gross salary → 6100 (full amount — always the expense)
Advance already given → reduces 2400 directly when advance was granted
Net paid = what physically leaves cash/bank

Salary Expense ↑  |  Salary Payable ↑ (on accrual)  |  Cash/Bank ↓ (on payment)
RULE

B8  —  Sale Return
B8
Sale Return (Credit Note)
WHAT
Customer returns goods. Revenue reversed. Stock restored at original FIFO purchase cost. Cash refunded or credit note issued.
JOURNAL
Reverse the revenue
DR  4000  Sales Revenue           = returned_net_amount  (ex-tax, after discounts)
DR  2200  Sales Tax Payable       = returned_tax
CR  1200  Accounts Receivable     = total_returned_invoice_amount
Restore inventory — oldest-first FIFO (same direction as sales)
DR  1100  Inventory Asset         = FIFO cost of returned items
CR  5000  Cost of Goods Sold      = FIFO cost  (COGS reversed)
If CASH REFUND given (separate entry)
DR  1200  Accounts Receivable     = refund_amount  (clears the credit)
CR  1000  Cash in Hand            = refund_amount
AFFECTS
Revenue ↓  |  Tax Payable ↓  |  Receivables ↓  |  Inventory ↑  |  COGS ↓  |  Cash ↓ (if cash refund)
RULE
Returned stock re-enters at original FIFO PURCHASE cost — not at the sale price.
Restore remaining_qty on the SAME batch the item was originally deducted from (sale_item_batches).
payment_allocations: update or reverse allocation for the original invoice.
B9  —  Purchase Return
B9
Purchase Return (Debit Note)
WHAT
You return goods to a supplier. Inventory decreases. Payable decreases (or cash refund received).
JOURNAL
Reduce inventory and payables
DR  2000  Accounts Payable        = cost_of_returned_items
CR  1100  Inventory Asset         = cost_of_returned_items
If supplier gives CASH REFUND
DR  1000  Cash in Hand            = refund_amount
CR  2000  Accounts Payable        = refund_amount

AFFECTS
Inventory ↓  |  Payables ↓  |  Cash ↑ (if cash refund)

payment_allocations: reverse or update allocation for original purchase invoice.
B10  —  Stock Adjustment  ★ FIX 5 APPLIED
B10
Stock Adjustment
WHAT
Manual correction to stock count — damage, theft, expiry write-off, audit variance. BOTH quantity AND financial value change.
JOURNAL
Stock DECREASE — uses oldest-first FIFO  ★ CORRECTED FROM v1.0
DR  6300  Stock Adjustment Loss   = qty_removed × oldest_batch_unit_cost
CR  1100  Inventory Asset         = qty_removed × oldest_batch_unit_cost
FifoService: remove from oldest batch first (ORDER BY created_at ASC)
REASON: expired/damaged goods ARE the oldest goods — this is consistent with sales FIFO
Stock INCREASE — creates a new batch
DR  1100  Inventory Asset         = qty_added × specified_cost
CR  4200  Stock Adjustment Gain   = qty_added × specified_cost
FifoService: create new inventory_batches record dated today
AFFECTS
Inventory Asset ↑ or ↓  |  Adjustment Gain/Loss ↑  |  Net Profit affected
RULE
★ v1.0 said 'newest batch backwards (reverse FIFO)' — THIS WAS WRONG. v2.0 corrects it.
ALL stock decreases use oldest-first (ASC). Sales (B1/B2), adjustments (B10), donations (B22) — same direction.
Journal entry AND inventory_batches update must happen in one DB::transaction().
Auto-heal must NOT be the mechanism for stock correction — use explicit B10 entries only.

B12

WHAT

B16
Manufacturing / Production Run
WHAT
Raw materials consumed. Finished product created. Labor cost allocated to inventory — NOT credited to Cash if the labor is already on the payroll.

RULE

  CALCULATED BALANCES
  C1–C26: Every balance — formula, source, and caching rules
No balance is stored as a mutable running total for financial reporting. All balances are calculated from the ledger or from snapshot caches that are guaranteed to mirror the ledger. Caching is permitted for performance — but invalidation rules are mandatory.
4.0  The payment_allocations Table  ★ NEW IN v2.0
This table was missing in v1.0. It is the architectural component that connects a payment (B4/B5 journal entry) to specific invoices. Without it, invoice payment_status badges are broken and aged receivables are wrong.
🆕 payment_allocations — schema definition
CREATE TABLE payment_allocations (
  id                      BIGINT UNSIGNED AUTO_INCREMENT PRIMARY KEY,
  journal_entry_id        BIGINT UNSIGNED NOT NULL,  -- the B4 or B5 journal entry
  sale_id                 BIGINT UNSIGNED NULL,      -- which sale invoice (if Payment In)
  purchase_id             BIGINT UNSIGNED NULL,      -- which purchase invoice (if Payment Out)
  allocated_amount        DECIMAL(15,2) NOT NULL,
  created_at              TIMESTAMP DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP,
  FOREIGN KEY (journal_entry_id) REFERENCES journal_entries(id),
  FOREIGN KEY (sale_id)          REFERENCES sales(id),
  FOREIGN KEY (purchase_id)      REFERENCES purchases(id)
);
CONSTRAINT: SUM(allocated_amount) for one journal_entry_id must equal the payment total.
INDEX on: journal_entry_id, sale_id, purchase_id
Use Case
Query Against payment_allocations
Sales invoice payment_status badge
SELECT SUM(allocated_amount) WHERE sale_id = X → compare to invoice total
Aged receivables per invoice
SELECT SUM(allocated_amount) WHERE sale_id = X → outstanding = invoice_total - paid
Supplier invoice payment_status
SELECT SUM(allocated_amount) WHERE purchase_id = X
Party statement per invoice
JOIN payment_allocations to sales/purchases for running balance per invoice
Unapplied payment credit
Payment total - SUM(allocations for this journal_entry_id) = unapplied credit
📐 payment_allocations rules
Every B4 Payment In must create at least one payment_allocations row (or the payment is 'on account')
Every B5 Payment Out must create at least one payment_allocations row
Partial payments: one row per invoice, allocated_amount = how much of this payment goes to that invoice
Unallocated credit (payment &gt; invoices): one row with sale_id = NULL, amount = excess — applies later
On allocating: SUM(all rows for this payment) must equal the payment journal entry total
4.1  The party_snapshots Table  ★ NEW IN v2.0 — Fix 3
Performance architecture for party balances. The ledger is the single source of truth. Snapshots are a performance cache — not the source. They are guaranteed to be up-to-date because they are rebuilt every time a new journal entry touches a party.
🆕 party_snapshots — schema definition
CREATE TABLE party_snapshots (
  id              BIGINT UNSIGNED AUTO_INCREMENT PRIMARY KEY,
  party_id        BIGINT UNSIGNED NOT NULL,
  account_code    CHAR(4) NOT NULL,  -- '1200' for customers, '2000' for suppliers
  cached_balance  DECIMAL(15,2) NOT NULL DEFAULT 0.00,
  last_journal_id BIGINT UNSIGNED NULL,  -- last journal entry that triggered rebuild
  last_updated    TIMESTAMP DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP ON UPDATE CURRENT_TIMESTAMP,
  PRIMARY KEY via (party_id, account_code)
);
-- Invalidation trigger: runs after every INSERT into journal_entries
-- where party_id IS NOT NULL
-- After posting any journal entry with party_id:
function rebuildPartySnapshot(party_id, account_code) {
    $fresh = DB::table('journal_items')
        -&gt;join('journal_entries', 'journal_items.journal_entry_id', '=', 'journal_entries.id')
        -&gt;join('accounts', 'journal_items.account_id', '=', 'accounts.id')
        -&gt;where('journal_entries.party_id', party_id)
        -&gt;where('accounts.code', account_code)
        -&gt;where('journal_entries.is_reversed', false)
        -&gt;selectRaw('SUM(debit) - SUM(credit) as balance')
        -&gt;value('balance') ?? 0.00;
    DB::table('party_snapshots')-&gt;updateOrInsert(
        ['party_id' =&gt; party_id, 'account_code' =&gt; account_code],
        ['cached_balance' =&gt; $fresh, 'last_journal_id' =&gt; $new_entry_id,
         'last_updated' =&gt; now()]
    );
}
-- Reading party balance (fast path):
function getPartyBalance(party_id, account_code) {
    $snap = DB::table('party_snapshots')
        -&gt;where('party_id', party_id)-&gt;where('account_code', account_code)-&gt;first();
    if ($snap) return $snap-&gt;cached_balance;
    // Fallback: live ledger query (first visit or cache miss)
    return rebuildPartySnapshot(party_id, account_code);
}
📐 party_snapshots rules
Snapshot is ALWAYS rebuilt immediately after any journal entry with that party_id is posted
Reading the balance: read from snapshot. If snapshot missing: fall back to live ledger query
The live ledger query is ALWAYS available as the fallback — snapshots never replace the truth
If a snapshot appears stale (last_journal_id doesn't match latest entry): rebuild it
On database restore / migration: truncate party_snapshots and rebuild all from ledger

  SECTION 6
  INVENTORY SYSTEMS
  E1–E5: FIFO rules v2.0 — unified direction for all stock decreases
E3  —  FIFO Direction Rule  ★ FIX 5 APPLIED
v1.0 had a contradiction: sales used oldest-first (ASC) while stock adjustments used newest-first (DESC / reverse FIFO). This is now corrected. ALL stock decreases — regardless of the reason — use oldest-first FIFO.
🚫 v1.0 was wrong — the contradiction
B1/B2 Sales: ORDER BY inventory_batches.created_at ASC  (oldest first)
B10 Stock Decrease: ORDER BY inventory_batches.created_at DESC  (newest first — WRONG)
Why this is wrong: expired goods, damaged goods, stolen goods ARE the oldest goods.
Writing off newest stock while keeping oldest stock inflates inventory value (old cheap stock
stays, expensive new stock leaves). This contradicts FIFO accounting entirely.
✅ v2.0 — one rule for all stock decreases
ALL stock out events use: ORDER BY inventory_batches.created_at ASC  (oldest first)
This applies to: B1 Sales, B2 POS Sales, B8 Sale Returns (deductions), B9 Purchase Returns,
  B10 Stock Adjustments (decreases), B16 Raw material consumption, B22 Charity donations
The only exception: B8/B9 RESTORES go back to the SPECIFIC original batch (not FIFO direction)
-- FifoService.deductStock() — used by ALL decrease operations
function deductStock(product_id, qty_needed, warehouse_id = null) {
    $batches = DB::table('inventory_batches')
        -&gt;where('product_id', product_id)
        -&gt;where('remaining_qty', '&gt;', 0)
        -&gt;when($warehouse_id, fn($q) =&gt; $q-&gt;where('warehouse_id', $warehouse_id))
        -&gt;whereNull('deleted_at')
        -&gt;orderBy('created_at', 'ASC')   // ← OLDEST FIRST, ALWAYS
        -&gt;get();
    $remaining = $qty_needed;
    $deductions = [];
    foreach ($batches as $batch) {
        if ($remaining &lt;= 0) break;
        $take = min($remaining, $batch-&gt;remaining_qty);
        $deductions[] = ['batch_id' =&gt; $batch-&gt;id, 'qty' =&gt; $take, 'unit_cost' =&gt; $batch-&gt;unit_cost];
        DB::table('inventory_batches')-&gt;where('id', $batch-&gt;id)
            -&gt;decrement('remaining_qty', $take);
        $remaining -= $take;
    }
    if ($remaining &gt; 0) throw new InsufficientStockException();
    return $deductions;  // used to build sale_item_batches and COGS journal entry
}

B30  —  Product Set Disassembly (Bought as Set, Sold Separately)  ★ NEW v3.0
B30
Product Set Disassembly (Bought as Set, Sold Separately)
TRIGGER
A bundled product (e.g. printer set) is purchased as one item but will be sold as individual components.
JOURNAL
On disassembly run: CR  1100  Inventory Asset  = total_set_fifo_cost  (remove the bundle)  |  DR  1100  Inventory Asset  = allocated_cost_per_component  (create individual component batches)  |  Each component batch unit_cost = total_set_cost x component_cost_allocation_percentage  |  Cost allocation percentages are defined in the Disassembly BOM — must sum to 100%
THE RULE

NOTE

B31  —  Unit of Measure Conversion (Buy in KG, Sell in Grams)  ★ NEW v3.0
B31
Unit of Measure Conversion (Buy in KG, Sell in Grams)
TRIGGER
A product is purchased in one unit (KG) but sold in a different unit (Grams). System handles the conversion.
JOURNAL
Purchase side (normal B2): stock arrives in base UOM (e.g. KG). Batch unit_cost stored per KG.  |  Sale side: user enters quantity in sale UOM (e.g. 500g). System converts: deducted_qty = sale_qty / conversion_factor  |  Example: sell 500g of rice. conversion_factor = 1000 (1 KG = 1000g). Deducted from batch = 0.5 KG.  |  COGS = deducted_qty_in_base_uom x batch_unit_cost_per_base_uom
THE RULE
Each product has ONE base UOM (stored in products.base_uom) — the unit used in inventory_batches  /  Each product can have multiple sale UOMs with a conversion_factor stored in product_uom_conversions table  /  product_uom_conversions: {product_id, sale_uom, conversion_factor} — e.g. {rice_id, grams, 1000}  /  If no conversion exists for the sale UOM selected, the system blocks the sale and prompts setup  /  Conversion is always: base_qty = sale_qty / conversion_factor — never the reverse on deduction
NOTE
The invoice shows the sale UOM quantity (e.g. 500g). Inventory batch records show base UOM deductions (0.5 KG).

  SECTION 9
  THE 18 LAWS
  v2.0 — expanded from 15. Every violation causes a specific failure.
These are not guidelines. A code review must reject any pull request that violates one of these laws. Every law traces directly to a failure mode.
🚫 LAW 1 — Every financial transaction posts a journal entry
No exceptions. Every B1–B24 transaction calls AccountingService::createEntry()
Failure mode: transaction invisible to P&amp;L, Trial Balance, Cash Flow, all ledger reports
🚫 LAW 2 — Journal entries are never deleted
To correct: post a reversal entry. Both entries stay in the database permanently.
Failure mode: broken audit trail, Trial Balance drift, historical reports wrong
🚫 LAW 3 — Every journal entry must be perfectly balanced
SUM(debit) = SUM(credit) to the last paisa. Engine validates before saving — throws if not.
Failure mode: single unbalanced entry permanently corrupts the Trial Balance
🚫 LAW 4 — All writes happen inside DB::transaction()
Business record + journal entries together. If any part fails, everything rolls back.
Failure mode: sale with no journal entry, or journal entry with no sale — broken data state
🚫 LAW 5 — Cash balance is always account 1000 from journal_items
Never from payments table. Never from custom formula. Never from bank_accounts.current_balance.
Failure mode: Cash shows different numbers on different screens
🚫 LAW 6 — bank_accounts.current_balance is a display cache only
Never used for financial calculations. Never updated by controller arithmetic.
Failure mode: bank balance desyncs from ledger silently
🚫 LAW 7 — parties.current_balance is a display cache only
Party balances come from party_snapshots → journal_items. Never manual arithmetic.
Failure mode: party balance wrong, aged reports wrong
🚫 LAW 8 — Tax is never included in Revenue
Account 4000 Revenue = net_sales (ex-tax). Tax → 2200 Sales Tax Payable.
Failure mode: P&amp;L overstates revenue, tax filing wrong
🚫 LAW 9 — COGS always uses FIFO batch cost from sale_item_batches
Never products.cost_price. Never a simple average. Always locked purchase batch cost.
Failure mode: COGS wrong, gross profit wrong, inventory value inflated
🚫 LAW 10 — Inventory Value always uses inventory_batches
SUM(remaining_qty × unit_cost) from inventory_batches WHERE remaining_qty &gt; 0.
Failure mode: Balance Sheet asset value wrong, Net Book Value wrong
🚫 LAW 11 — payment_status badge comes from payment_allocations only
invoice_total - SUM(payment_allocations.allocated_amount WHERE sale_id = X) = outstanding.
Failure mode: badges show wrong status, aged receivables wrong, party statements wrong
🚫 LAW 12 — Salaries use B7, never B5
Salary = Expense (6100). Payment Out = reduces Payable (2000). These are different accounts.
Failure mode: salary invisible in P&amp;L, Accounts Payable inflated
🚫 LAW 13 — Customer advances are liabilities (2100), never revenue
Revenue recognised only on delivery. Advance received = DR Cash / CR 2100.
Failure mode: P&amp;L overstates revenue before delivery, Balance Sheet wrong
🚫 LAW 14 — ALL stock decreases use oldest-first FIFO  ★ FIX 5
ORDER BY inventory_batches.created_at ASC for: sales, adjustments, write-offs, donations.
Failure mode: inventory value distorted, COGS inconsistent, Balance Sheet asset wrong
🚫 LAW 15 — Fixed assets go to account 1500, not 6000 expenses
Purchasing equipment → DR 1500 Fixed Assets. Depreciation → DR 6600 / CR 1510.
Failure mode: P&amp;L overstates expenses in purchase period, Balance Sheet missing assets
🚫 LAW 16 — Manufacturing salaried labor uses Path 2B (Salary Payable, not Cash)  ★ FIX 1
Salaried employees: DR 6410 Applied Labor / CR 2400 Salary Payable. Never CR Cash twice.
Failure mode: double-counted cash outflow, inflated manufacturing costs
🚫 LAW 17 — Account 7000 Opening Balance Equity must net to zero after B19
SUM(all DR to 7000) = SUM(all CR from 7000) after all opening entries are posted.
Failure mode: Balance Sheet doesn't balance on day one, all subsequent reports wrong
🚫 LAW 18 — party_snapshots are rebuilt immediately after every party-linked journal entry
Any INSERT to journal_entries with a party_id triggers rebuildPartySnapshot().
Failure mode: party balances stale, performance degrades to raw ledger scan at scale
